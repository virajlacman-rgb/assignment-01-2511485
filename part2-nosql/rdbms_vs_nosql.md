## RDBMS vs NoSQL

Relational databases (RDBMS) store data in structured tables with predefined schemas and relationships using foreign keys. They are ideal for transactional systems and structured data.

NoSQL databases store data in flexible formats such as documents, key-value pairs, or graphs. They are designed for scalability, large datasets, and semi-structured or unstructured data.

RDBMS ensures strong consistency and normalization, while NoSQL prioritizes scalability and flexible schema design.

In this assignment, the relational schema was converted into a document-based model where customer data, orders, and products are stored together inside a single document.
